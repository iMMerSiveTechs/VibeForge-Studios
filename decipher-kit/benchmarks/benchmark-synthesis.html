Key Findings

What the Benchmark Proves

1
No AI broke 54% on personalized handwriting. The best raw AI (no glyph map) hit 50%. This gap IS the market for DecipherKit — every major AI gets your handwriting wrong half the time.
2
The glyph map works — but needs a leash. AI #2 (with glyph map) was the only one to get "Rep Plan" and "Tracks" right. But it also caused 3 hallucinations via context bleed. The confidence gate (v0.3) is the fix.
3
Zero hallucinations > high accuracy. AI #3 scored lowest (36%) but never fabricated a single word. In production, a wrong read you can correct is better than a confident hallucination you can't detect.
4
Confidence without accuracy is the worst outcome. AI #4 marked wrong reads as HIGH confidence, built themes on fabricated words, and listed hallucinated items as action items. DecipherKit must prevent this failure mode above all others.
5
Notepad (structured, multi-column) is 2-3x harder than sticky notes (large, isolated text) for every AI tested. This is where the glyph map adds the most value.
6
Cross-image synthesis is broken everywhere. Same stickies appear in multiple photos — no AI fully leveraged this to resolve ambiguous reads. DecipherKit's multi-image protocol is a genuine differentiator.
7
Structural notation is invisible to raw AI. Phase separators (——), flanking dashes (— word —), arrows (←), parenthetical qualifiers ((Audit)) — 3 of 4 AIs ignored or misinterpreted these. They carry real semantic meaning in JayTee's system.
8
Edge scanning is a universal gap. Procurement OS was visible at the edge of multiple images. 0 for 4 AIs caught it. The v0.3 edge scanning mandate addresses this directly.
